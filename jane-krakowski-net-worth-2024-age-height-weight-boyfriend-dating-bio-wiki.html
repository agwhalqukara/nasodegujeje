Early Life

Jane Krakowski is born on 11th October 1968 in Parsippany-Troy Hills, New Jersey, the United States of America to Edward and Barbara Krakowski. She belongs to Canadian, Polish, French, and Scottish ancestry. Her father was a chemical engineer, and her mother was a college theatre instructor cum teacher. She lived with her parents, along with her elder brother.

This talented actress was influenced and inspired by her father while choosing her career. Her mother was a producing artistic director of Women’s Theater Company. Jane grew up in the theatre ambiance. Her parents brought her along with them while they went for work. Jane learned ballet at the age of 4 only. Then she started Broadway dancing. Jane attended the Professional Children’s School and Parsippany High School in New York City and then joined Rutgers University, New Brunswick.

Career

Jane’s debut film is National Lampoon’s Vacation, which has been released in 1983. Awards & Achievements
